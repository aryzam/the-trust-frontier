Methodology & data sources

Protection scores are constructed from legal facts: does binding law exist, is it enforced, what individual remedies exist, what penalties apply. They are defensible within ±10 points and cited to primary legal sources on each row.

Public trust figures are real survey data — not estimates. Primary source: Pew Research Center, "How People Around the World View AI," October 2025, n=28,333 adults across 25 countries (Jan–Apr 2025). UK figures also draw on Ada Lovelace Institute / Alan Turing Institute nationally representative survey, March 2025, n=3,513. US figures from Pew and Quinnipiac University (April 2025).

The Trust Frontier gap replaces a fabricated numerical score. It describes the observable mismatch between public trust and actual binding protection using specific evidence. It is qualitative, honest, and designed to provoke rather than to measure. Challenges welcomed at thetrustfrontier.substack.com.
